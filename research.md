I am an evolutionary biologist interested in linking empirical patterns of genomic variation with theories of adaptation. I typically combine computational and experimental approaches. I have previously used genome-wide association studies to investigate the genetic basis of sex-specific fitness in D. melanogaster, population genomic methods to identify signatures of sexually antagonistic selection, and experiments to test the predicted effects of individual candidate genes. I am also interested in a number of related questions, including: contrasts between X chromosome and autosomal evolution, processes that underpin the evolution of inversions, and the role of dominance in adaptation.
